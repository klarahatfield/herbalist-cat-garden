The mist here lifts slowly. Always slowly. Never the abrupt shock of waking — but the unhurried uncurling of a petal that has waited all night for permission to open.
I am Baba. Guardian of this place. And you, for reasons the garden has not yet explained to me, have found your way here.
Step through the Purr-Forest on the western border. The ancient oaks won't startle you — they never do. They simply hum, low and steady, like a cat settled deep into its favorite chair.
The soil here is no ordinary earth. It holds memories.
To the north, the Velvet Moss Hills look like nothing more than a soft emerald carpet. But kneel down. Press your palm flat against the moss. Feel that? Beneath your hand, roots run deeper than any map can show.
At the center, the Dreaming Bloom-Beds wait. The orange blossoms must be harvested only by the light of a specific moon — too early and they're hollow, too late and they've already given their gift to the air.
But the true heartbeat of this garden? The Lunar Lure Lagoon. Its water is pearlescent blue on calm nights, perfectly still, perfectly clear. I have learned to move like water myself on those nights. Slow. Deliberate. Barely breathing.
This map I've drawn for you is not a static picture. It is a breathing record — grown more than drawn, revised more than planned. Every path you see has been walked in darkness at least once. Every label was earned.
Now. The Lagoon is still tonight. The Bloom-Beds are nearly ready.
Which calls to you first — the water, or the dream?
